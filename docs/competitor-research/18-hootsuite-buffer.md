# Hootsuite & Buffer (Social media scheduling)

> Two competing approaches to the same job: queue social posts, schedule them
> across networks, and report on engagement. Buffer is the lean
> small-business tool; Hootsuite is the enterprise-grade suite. The price gap
> is roughly 13x for similar core scheduling.

## A. Core value prop

**Buffer:** "Plan, schedule, and analyze social media posts across networks
from one place — affordably, even free." Lean composer + queue + light
analytics, designed for solo creators and small teams.

**Hootsuite:** "Manage your entire social presence at scale" — composer,
streams (real-time monitoring), social listening (mentions/sentiment),
inbox (DMs and comments unified), analytics, employee advocacy, ad
management. Built for agencies and enterprise marketing departments.

---

## Buffer

Buffer's product personality is "boring and reliable." The core flow is the
**Queue**: configure posting times per channel, drop posts in, and they go
out in slot order. The composer is a single textarea with a tabbed
per-network preview — you write once, then tweak the LinkedIn or X variant
inline. Image/video uploads support per-network aspect ratios. Their hashtag
manager and saved-prompts library are AI-assisted but optional. The free
tier is genuinely usable: 3 channels, 10 scheduled posts per channel, a
basic 30-day analytics window, and one user. Buffer's analytics emphasize
per-post performance (reach, engagement rate, link clicks) rather than
campaign-level rollups. Notable UI patterns to copy: the
**queue-as-grid** (slots are visually empty cells you fill, not abstract
records); the **per-network preview tab** (the same draft renders four ways
in real time); and the **tag system on posts** for after-the-fact filtering.
Pricing: Free, then $5/channel/mo (Essentials), $10/channel/mo (Team).
For Zach, Buffer's free tier covers Blu's BBQ today (Instagram + Facebook +
maybe X); upgrading to Essentials at $15/mo for three channels is trivial.

## Hootsuite

Hootsuite's bet is that social-media management is a *whole job* deserving
a whole-suite product, not a feature. The composer is similar to Buffer's,
but the centerpiece is the **Streams view**: a multi-column dashboard
(à la TweetDeck) where each column is a saved query — mentions of @brand,
DMs, comments on the latest post, hashtags being monitored. Each column is
an inbox a CMM can clear. The **Inbox** unifies DMs + comments across all
networks into one queue with auto-tagging and saved replies. **OwlyWriter
AI** drafts captions, suggests hashtags, and recommends best-time-to-post
windows trained on the account's history. **Social listening** (Blue Silk
AI) tracks brand mentions and sentiment across the open web — features
nobody at Blu's scale needs. The price reflects scope: Standard $99/mo,
Advanced $249/mo, Enterprise custom — versus a 30-day trial only. Pattern
worth borrowing even at small scale: the **Streams metaphor** maps cleanly
to "multiple saved filters living side by side," which is exactly what
the inquiries page already wants (overdue, awaiting-reply, needs-review).
Pattern to skip: the heavy enterprise inbox + listening stack — overkill
and expensive.

## A short comparison table

| Dimension | Buffer | Hootsuite |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Permanent, 3 channels, 10 queued posts | 30-day trial only |
| Solo entry price | $5/channel/mo | $99/mo flat |
| Composer | Per-network preview tabs | Per-network preview + media library |
| Best-time-to-post | Yes (Essentials+) | Yes (all paid) |
| Streams / monitoring | No | Yes (core feature) |
| Unified inbox | Light | Heavy (with auto-tagging) |
| Social listening | No | Yes (Blue Silk AI) |
| Approval workflows | Team plan | Advanced+ |
| AI assist | AI Assistant (paid) | OwlyWriter AI (paid) |
| Best for | Solo / small team | Agency / enterprise |

**Recommendation for Blu's BBQ:** Use Buffer free tier for actual posting
today. Don't build social scheduling into the Blu's app — the buy-vs-build
math is laughable when Buffer is free. **Do** copy Buffer's queue-grid and
per-network-preview UI patterns into the future "outbound campaign" module
where we schedule emails + SMS + (eventually) social posts to a customer
segment.
